Francisco de Assis França

The mind behind Manguebeat

Who was Francisco de Assis França?

Francisco de Assis França, known as Chico Science, were a brasilian musician responsible for creating the Manguebeat movement in the middle of 1990, the last biggest musical revolution from Brazil. Chico was the singer and leader from the band Chico Science & Nação Zumbi, recorded only two albuns: "Da Lama ao Caos" and "Afrociberdelia", as he was a victim of car accident in 1997. His both albuns were included in 100 best albums of Brazilian music by Rolling Stone magazine and his artistic contribution persist and it's been unfolding until today.

Trajectory

  • Born on 03/13/1966 in Olinda, Pernambuco, Brazil
  • In 1984, he joined one of the main dancing groups of Recife, Legião Hip-Hop.
  • In 1987, he formed his first musical group, called "Orla Orbe", that used to play black music, but the group dismissed before completing one year.
  • After that, he formed the band "Loustal", in honor of French comic artist Jacques de Loustal, that played a mix of 1960's rock with funk and hip-hop.
  • The fusion of brasilian's northeastern rythmics, specially with Maracatu, began in 1991, when he came into contact with the afro block Lamento Negro, from Peixinhos, a suburb of Olinda, which was doing popular education work on the outskirts of greater Recife, and brought together folkloric rhythms, such as rural maracatu and côco de roda with samba-reggae.
  • Com a fusão do Loustal e o Lamento Negro surgiu o grupo “Chico Science e Lamento Negro”, que depois foi batizado com o nome “Chico Science & Nação Zumbi”. A estreia da banda aconteceu em junho de 1991.
  • In 1993, a quick tour of São Paulo and Belo Horizonte caught the attention of the media.
  • The first album was released in 1994 (Da Lama ao Caos), which featured the songs “A Praieira” and “A Cidade”, which were part of the soundtrack for the soap operas Tropicaliente and Irmãos Coragem, respectively. The album was well received by critics and promoted the band nationally.
  • The second album, Afrociberdelia, from 1996, more pop and electronic, confirmed the innovative tendency of Chico Science and Nação Zumbi, who toured Europe, where they met Os Paralamas do Sucesso, and the United States, where they were a success with the public and critics and they played together with Gilberto Gil. Gil considered Chico Science (along with the Bahian group Olodum) as "the most important thing that has emerged in Brazilian music in the last twenty years". The song “Maracatu Atômico”, a song by Jorge Mautner and Nelson Jacobina, which was a hit in 1973, performed by Gilberto Gil, became the group's anthem.
  • February 2, 1997 would go down as the most devastating day in the history of manguebeat. At the end of that afternoon, the car driven by Chico Science collided with a pole on the highway between Recife and Olinda, taking the life of the singer and composer, on the eve of turning 31 years old. Science was still rescued by a police officer who was passing by on a bus and took him to Hospital da Restauração, but he did not resist and arrived at the hospital dead. The burial took place on Monday, February 3, 1997, at the Santo Amaro Cemetery, located in Recife.
  • His two albums were included in the list of the 100 best albums of Brazilian music by Rolling Stone magazine, drawn up based on a vote with 60 journalists, producers and scholars of Brazilian music: Da Lama ao Caos was in 13th position and Afrociberdelia in 18th. In October 2008, Rolling Stone magazine promoted the List of the One Hundred Greatest Artists of Brazilian Music, in which Chico Science occupied 16th place.
  • Although sad, Chico Science's early death strengthened the Manguebeat Movement. Nação Zumbi released a double CD in 1998, with new songs and live versions remixed by DJs. Their version of the song "Maracatu Atômico" was the clip that closed the activities of MTV Brasil, by the group Abril, on September 30, 2013.
